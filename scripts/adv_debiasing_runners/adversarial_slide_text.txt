================================================================
SLIDE 12 — Adversarial De-biasing: Sweep over lambda
================================================================

Idea:
  Train classifier against an adversary that predicts dialect
  from the output. Lambda weights the gradient-reversal term,
  enforcing Equalized Odds [Zhang et al., 2018].

Key Takeaways:
  - Small lambda -> near-baseline: large FPR/FNR gaps.
  - Moderate lambda (0.15-0.25): both gaps collapse, F1 ~ 0.82.
  - Large lambda (>= 0.6): unstable - FPR spikes, F1 drops.
  - Fairness does NOT improve monotonically with lambda.

Best (lambda=0.25, adv_c=3.0, thresh-tuned):
  FPR_AAE 0.185 | FPR gap 0.033
  FNR_AAE 0.161 | FNR gap 0.046
  F1 82.3       | Acc 82.6


================================================================
SLIDE 13 — Adversarial De-biasing: Sweep over adv_c
================================================================

Idea:
  Fix lambda = 0.25; vary the adversary's capacity (adv_c =
  inverse-L2 of the LR adversary).

Key Takeaways:
  - Weak (<= 0.5): noisy, F1 drops.
  - Mid (c=1.0): worst cell - AAE FPR spikes to 0.52.
  - Strong (c=3.0): equilibrium; balanced FPR, stable F1.
  - adv_c shows a non-monotonic / bathtub response.
  - adv_c explains only ~1% of F1 variance (vs lambda's ~54%):
    it controls STABILITY, not the accuracy-fairness trade-off.

Takeaway:
  c = 3.0 is the smallest capacity that converges cleanly.
  Used in all subsequent results.


================================================================
SLIDE 14 — Impact of Threshold Tuning
================================================================

Idea:
  Post-hoc: pick per-group thresholds (t_AAE, t_SAE) on val to
  minimize FPR gap under an F1 floor [Hardt et al., 2016].

Key Takeaways:
  - FPR: AAE halves (0.370 -> 0.185); gap drops 0.215 -> 0.033.
  - FNR: AAE rises (+0.065); gap shrinks 0.109 -> 0.046 (balanced).
  - F1 cost: only -0.008.
  - Adversarial training aligns score distributions; threshold
    tuning converts that alignment into fair decisions.

One-liner:
  Aligned scores (adversarial) + fair decisions (threshold tuning)
  = balanced errors at ~1pt F1 cost.
